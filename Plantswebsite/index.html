Frequently Asked Questions:

My flowers are falling off or dying?

Flower drop or wilting can result from various factors such as overwatering, inadequate light, or improper nutrition. Ensure balanced care, including proper watering and light exposure, to sustain healthy blooms.

What causes leaves to become pale?

Leaf paleness often indicates insufficient light exposure or nutrient deficiencies, particularly nitrogen. Adjusting light levels and ensuring a balanced fertilizer regimen can help restore vibrant leaf color.

What causes brown crispy leaves?

Brown, crispy leaves are often a result of underwatering, low humidity, or exposure to direct sunlight. Ensure consistent watering, increase humidity levels, and consider relocating the plant to a spot with indirect sunlight to prevent further leaf damage.

How do i choose a plant?

Select a plant by considering factors such as light conditions in your space, your level of experience with plant care, and your aesthetic preferences. Choose plants that thrive in your environment and match your care abilities to ensure successful growth and enjoyment.

How do I change the pots?

To repot a plant, select a new pot slightly larger than the current one, ensuring it has drainage holes. Gently remove the plant from its old pot, loosen the root ball, and place it in the center of the new pot. Fill in with fresh potting mix, ensuring the plant sits at the same depth as before. Water thoroughly and place in an appropriate location for optimal growth.

Why are gnats flying around my plant?

Gnats are attracted to moist soil, often indicating overwatering or poor drainage. Allow the soil to dry out between waterings, improve air circulation around the plant, and consider using organic soil amendments to deter gnats.
